Things to know

  • Payment on account: After your first Self Assessment, HMRC usually splits your bill into advance payments — 50% due 31 January and 50% due 31 July. Budget for this in year two.
  • Trading allowance: If your self-employment income is under £1,000 you may not need to register or pay tax on it.
  • PAYE estimate: Assumes a standard 1257L tax code. If HMRC has adjusted your code (e.g. due to benefits or high income), your actual PAYE may differ.
  • Personal allowance taper: If your total income exceeds £100,000 your personal allowance reduces — the effective tax rate between £100k–£125,140 is 60%.
  • Scotland: Scottish taxpayers have different income tax rates — this calculator applies England, Wales & Northern Ireland rates only.
  • This is an estimate. Rates shown are for 2025/26. Always verify with HMRC or a qualified accountant.
